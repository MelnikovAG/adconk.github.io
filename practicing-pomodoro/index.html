What is Pomodoro?


  • A Time Management Technique
  • A Productivity Goal Tracker
  • A Learning Tool about yourself and your environment

When and where do I use Pomodoro?


  • During "Me" time: Identify things you want to get done independently of your team
  • Bad time: Running errands, out and about, planned meeting times with others

How do I start using Pomodoro?


  1. Make a tasklist - for the afternoon
  2. 45 mins / Work - this is a pomodoro
  3. keep tallies as you work - item(s) complete, internal interuptions, and external interuptions
  4. 15 mins / Break - drinks, restroom, stretch, socialize
  5. Repeat steps 2. to 4.

Why am I doing Pomodoro?


  1. To intensify productivity timeframes
  2. To feel a sense of accomplishment
  3. To learn how you interrupt yourself and improve over time
  4. To learn how your team interrupts you and present a better team management approach to your leaders through data

Background


  • Pomodoro Technique was published in 2006 by Francesco Cirillo
  • Conceptualized in the 80s
  • Developed during a time of low productivity and high confusion
